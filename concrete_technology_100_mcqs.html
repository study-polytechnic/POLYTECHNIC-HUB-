SBTE Concrete Technology - 100 MCQs

1. Which Bogue's compound in cement is responsible for early strength development?
A) Dicalcium Silicate (C2S)B) Tricalcium Silicate (C3S)C) Tricalcium Aluminate (C3A)D) Tetracalcium Aluminoferrite (C4AF)
Ans: B) Tricalcium Silicate (C3S)
Tricalcium Silicate (C3S) hydrates rapidly and contributes primarily to the early strength (within the first 7 days) of cement.
2. The resistance of an aggregate to wear and tear is determined by which test?
A) Crushing TestB) Impact TestC) Abrasion TestD) Soundness Test
Ans: C) Abrasion Test
Abrasion tests (like the Los Angeles test) measure the hardness or resistance to wear and tear (attrition and abrasion) of aggregates.
3. Separation of water and cement paste from the freshly mixed concrete is known as:
A) SegregationB) BleedingC) CreepD) Shrinkage
Ans: B) Bleeding
Bleeding is a form of segregation where water, being the lightest ingredient, rises to the surface of freshly placed concrete.
4. According to IS 10262:2019, the target mean strength (fck') for concrete mix design is calculated as:
A) fck + 1.65 × sB) fck - 1.65 × sC) fck + 1.15 × sD) fck + 0.5 × s
Ans: A) fck + 1.65 × s
The target mean compressive strength is given by fck' = fck + 1.65s, where fck is characteristic strength and 's' is the standard deviation.
5. Which admixture is added to concrete to delay its setting time, especially in hot weather?
A) AcceleratorsB) RetardersC) Air-entraining agentsD) Superplasticizers
Ans: B) Retarders
Retarders slow down the hydration process, extending the setting time of concrete. This is highly useful for hot weather concreting to prevent premature setting.
6. The standard consistency of cement is determined using which apparatus?
A) Le-Chatelier FlaskB) Slump ConeC) Vicat ApparatusD) Blaine's Apparatus
Ans: C) Vicat Apparatus
The Vicat apparatus is used to find the standard consistency, as well as the initial and final setting times of cement paste.
7. Bulking of sand is maximum when the moisture content is approximately:
A) 1% to 2%B) 4% to 5%C) 10% to 12%D) 15% to 20%
Ans: B) 4% to 5%
Moisture forms a film around sand particles, pushing them apart. This volume increase (bulking) is maximum at around 4% to 5% moisture content.
8. As per IS 456:2000, what is the minimum grade of concrete recommended for reinforced concrete work?
A) M10B) M15C) M20D) M25
Ans: C) M20
IS 456 specifies that the minimum grade of concrete for Reinforced Cement Concrete (RCC) should not be less than M20.
9. The stripping time for formwork of vertical surfaces (like columns and walls) under normal circumstances is:
A) 16 to 24 hoursB) 3 daysC) 7 daysD) 14 days
Ans: A) 16 to 24 hours
As per IS 456, formwork for vertical surfaces of columns, walls, and beams can generally be removed after 16 to 24 hours.
10. Self-Compacting Concrete (SCC) flows under its own weight and does not require:
A) CementB) WaterC) SuperplasticizersD) Mechanical Vibration
Ans: D) Mechanical Vibration
SCC is a highly flowable, non-segregating concrete that spreads into place and fully fills the formwork without any mechanical vibration.
11. The fineness of cement is determined by measuring its:
A) VolumeB) Specific GravityC) Specific Surface AreaD) Compressive Strength
Ans: C) Specific Surface Area
Fineness is a measure of the particle size of cement. It is usually tested using Blaine's air permeability apparatus, which measures the specific surface area (cm²/g).
12. An aggregate is said to be flaky if its least dimension is less than:
A) 0.6 times the mean dimensionB) 1.8 times the mean dimensionC) 0.5 times the mean dimensionD) 0.8 times the mean dimension
Ans: A) 0.6 times the mean dimension
An aggregate particle is classified as flaky if its thickness (least dimension) is less than 0.6 (or 3/5) of its mean sieve dimension.
13. Which test is considered most suitable for assessing the workability of very stiff concrete?
A) Slump TestB) Compaction Factor TestC) Vee-Bee Consistometer TestD) Flow Table Test
Ans: C) Vee-Bee Consistometer Test
The Vee-Bee test measures the time (in seconds) required for a concrete cone to slump into a cylinder under vibration. It is highly accurate for stiff and extremely dry mixes.
14. During mix design, the water-cement ratio is chosen based on:
A) Target Mean Strength and Durability requirementsB) Size of aggregate onlyC) Type of formworkD) Mixing time
Ans: A) Target Mean Strength and Durability requirements
The w/c ratio dictates both the strength (Abrams' law) and durability. The lower of the two values determined from strength graphs and durability tables (IS 456) is selected.
15. Fly ash, when used as a mineral admixture in concrete, primarily improves:
A) Early strengthB) Workability and long-term strengthC) Rate of hydrationD) Aggregate hardness
Ans: B) Workability and long-term strength
Fly ash acts as a pozzolan. Its spherical particles act like ball bearings (improving workability), and its delayed pozzolanic reaction significantly increases long-term strength and reduces permeability.
16. Which test evaluates the soundness of cement?
A) Vicat's TestB) Le-Chatelier TestC) Blaine's TestD) Briquette Test
Ans: B) Le-Chatelier Test
The Le-Chatelier apparatus measures the expansion of cement paste upon hydration. Unsoundness is usually caused by excess free lime and magnesia.
17. Coarse aggregate generally refers to aggregate particles that are retained on which IS Sieve?
A) 2.36 mmB) 4.75 mmC) 10 mmD) 75 microns
Ans: B) 4.75 mm
According to IS 383, aggregates retaining on a 4.75 mm IS Sieve are classified as coarse aggregates, while those passing through it are fine aggregates.
18. Increasing the water-cement ratio in a concrete mix leads to:
A) Higher strength, lower workabilityB) Lower strength, higher workabilityC) Higher strength, higher durabilityD) No change in properties
Ans: B) Lower strength, higher workability
Excess water pushes cement particles apart, leaving capillary voids after evaporation. This drastically lowers compressive strength but makes the fresh mix easier to flow (higher workability).
19. Construction joints in a simply supported beam should ideally be provided at:
A) The supportsB) The mid-spanC) Points of minimum shearD) Points of maximum bending moment
Ans: C) Points of minimum shear (near mid-span)
To minimize structural weakness, construction joints should be located where the shear force is minimum, which is typically near the middle of the span in a simply supported beam.
20. Concrete poured in freezing weather conditions is susceptible to damage due to:
A) Rapid evaporation of waterB) Freezing of capillary waterC) Accelerated setting timeD) Excessive shrinkage
Ans: B) Freezing of capillary water
In cold weather, if the water inside fresh concrete freezes, it expands by about 9%. This internal pressure can disrupt the matrix and cause permanent loss of strength.
21. High Alumina Cement (HAC) is manufactured by fusing limestone with:
A) SilicaB) BauxiteC) GypsumD) Iron oxide
Ans: B) Bauxite
High Alumina Cement is produced by melting a mixture of limestone and bauxite (aluminum ore). It is highly resistant to chemical attacks and withstands high temperatures.
22. The aggregate impact value indicates its resistance to:
A) Gradual crushingB) WeatheringC) Sudden shock or impactD) Chemical attack
Ans: C) Sudden shock or impact
The impact test evaluates the toughness of an aggregate, which is its ability to resist sudden, dynamic loads or shocks.
23. For testing the compressive strength of concrete, the standard size of the cube mould used in India is:
A) 100 mm x 100 mm x 100 mmB) 150 mm x 150 mm x 150 mmC) 200 mm x 200 mm x 200 mmD) 70.6 mm x 70.6 mm x 70.6 mm
Ans: B) 150 mm x 150 mm x 150 mm
As per IS 516, the standard concrete specimen for compressive strength testing is a 150 mm cube. (70.6 mm cubes are used for cement mortar).
24. The main objective of curing concrete is to:
A) Allow evaporation of excess waterB) Prevent the loss of moisture required for hydrationC) Increase the temperature of concreteD) Make the surface smooth
Ans: B) Prevent the loss of moisture required for hydration
Curing maintains a favorable moisture and temperature environment for concrete, ensuring continuous hydration of cement and maximizing final strength and durability.
25. Rebound hammer test is a method of Non-Destructive Testing primarily used to assess:
A) Internal flawsB) Surface hardness and probable compressive strengthC) Rebar corrosionD) Moisture content
Ans: B) Surface hardness and probable compressive strength
The Schmidt Rebound Hammer measures the elastic rebound of a spring-loaded mass impacting the surface, providing a correlation to the surface hardness and compressive strength.
26. The initial setting time of Ordinary Portland Cement (OPC) should not be less than:
A) 10 minutesB) 30 minutesC) 60 minutesD) 600 minutes
Ans: B) 30 minutes
According to IS specifications, the initial setting time of OPC should be at least 30 minutes to allow sufficient time for mixing, transporting, and placing the concrete.
27. Which IS Code provides guidelines for Concrete Mix Proportioning?
A) IS 456B) IS 383C) IS 10262D) IS 800
Ans: C) IS 10262
IS 10262:2019 is the Indian Standard for Concrete Mix Proportioning—Guidelines. IS 456 is for general reinforced concrete, and IS 383 is for aggregates.
28. Superplasticizers are chemical admixtures that:
A) Entrain air bubblesB) Accelerate setting timeC) Drastically reduce water requirement without loss of workabilityD) Add color to concrete
Ans: C) Drastically reduce water requirement without loss of workability
Superplasticizers (High Range Water Reducers) disperse cement particles effectively, allowing up to 30% reduction in water content while maintaining a flowing, workable mix.
29. Formwork made of plywood is often preferred over steel for:
A) Infinite reusabilityB) High initial costC) Creating smooth finishes and complex architectural shapesD) Better heat conduction
Ans: C) Creating smooth finishes and complex architectural shapes
Plywood is easy to cut into complex curves and provides a very smooth surface finish. However, it has fewer reuse cycles compared to steel shuttering.
30. What is the typical temperature range inside the rotary kiln during cement manufacturing?
A) 500°C - 800°CB) 900°C - 1100°CC) 1400°C - 1500°CD) 2000°C - 2500°C
Ans: C) 1400°C - 1500°C
In the burning zone of the rotary kiln, raw materials are heated to about 1400°C - 1500°C to form cement clinkers through a process called calcination and sintering.
31. Fineness Modulus of fine aggregate (sand) typically ranges between:
A) 1.0 to 1.5B) 2.2 to 3.2C) 4.0 to 5.0D) 6.0 to 8.0
Ans: B) 2.2 to 3.2
Fineness modulus represents the average size of particles. Fine sand ranges from 2.2-2.6, medium from 2.6-2.9, and coarse sand from 2.9-3.2.
32. The process of keeping concrete moist for a certain period after placing is called:
A) BatchingB) CompactingC) CuringD) Bleeding
Ans: C) Curing
Curing ensures that the concrete retains enough moisture for the cement hydration reaction to complete, leading to optimal strength development.
33. The compressive strength of concrete is largely dependent on:
A) WorkabilityB) Aggregate shapeC) Water-cement ratioD) Mixing time
Ans: C) Water-cement ratio
According to Duff Abrams' Law, for fully compacted concrete, the compressive strength is inversely proportional to the water-cement ratio.
34. During concrete mix design, the volume of coarse aggregate is decided based on:
A) Nominal maximum size of aggregate and grading zone of fine aggregateB) Cement contentC) Water content onlyD) Admixture dosage
Ans: A) Nominal maximum size of aggregate and grading zone of fine aggregate
IS 10262 provides tables that dictate the proportion of coarse aggregate based on the maximum size of the coarse aggregate (e.g., 20mm) and the specific grading zone of the sand (Zones I to IV).
35. Extreme hot weather concreting can lead to which major defect?
A) Delayed setting timeB) Plastic shrinkage crackingC) Frost damageD) Increased long-term strength
Ans: B) Plastic shrinkage cracking
High temperatures, low humidity, and high winds cause rapid evaporation of surface moisture before the concrete has set, resulting in tensile stresses and plastic shrinkage cracks.
36. Gypsum is added to cement clinker during grinding to:
A) Provide colorB) Increase early strengthC) Retard the initial setting timeD) Increase fineness
Ans: C) Retard the initial setting time
Without gypsum, the C3A in cement reacts violently with water causing a "flash set" (immediate stiffening). Gypsum (3-5%) delays this reaction, giving time for placement.
37. The elongation index of an aggregate is the percentage by weight of particles whose greatest dimension is greater than:
A) 0.6 times their mean dimensionB) 1.8 times their mean dimensionC) 1.5 times their mean dimensionD) 2.0 times their mean dimension
Ans: B) 1.8 times their mean dimension
Elongated particles are those whose length is substantially greater than their width. Mathematically, it is defined as a length greater than 1.8 (or 9/5) times the mean dimension.
38. The slump value for concrete used in normal RCC work (like slabs and beams) generally lies between:
A) 0 to 25 mmB) 50 to 100 mmC) 150 to 200 mmD) > 200 mm
Ans: B) 50 to 100 mm
A medium workability (slump of 50-100 mm) is ideal for heavily reinforced sections like slabs, beams, and columns to ensure easy flow without segregation.
39. In Mix Design, M30 grade concrete denotes a characteristic compressive strength of 30 N/mm² at:
A) 7 daysB) 14 daysC) 28 daysD) 90 days
Ans: C) 28 days
The characteristic compressive strength (fck) of concrete is universally tested and specified at the age of 28 days of standard curing.
40. Geopolymer concrete replaces ordinary Portland cement entirely with:
A) Silica fumeB) Lime and gypsumC) Source materials rich in Silicon and Aluminum activated by alkaline liquidsD) Epoxy resins
Ans: C) Source materials rich in Silicon and Aluminum activated by alkaline liquids
Geopolymer concrete is an eco-friendly alternative that uses industrial byproducts (like fly ash or slag) activated by alkaline solutions (like NaOH) instead of traditional OPC.
41. Which test evaluates the tensile strength of concrete indirectly?
A) Cube testB) Split cylinder testC) Slump testD) Rebound hammer test
Ans: B) Split cylinder test
Concrete is very weak in tension. The Split Cylinder Test places a cylindrical specimen horizontally and applies compressive force, causing indirect tensile splitting along the diameter.
42. The water absorption of good quality coarse aggregate should generally not exceed:
A) 0.5%B) 5%C) 10%D) 20%
Ans: B) 5%
Good quality aggregates should have a low water absorption value, typically restricted to a maximum of 5% by weight, to ensure durability and frost resistance.
43. Segregation in concrete can be caused by:
A) Excessive vibrationB) Dropping concrete from a large heightC) Too much water in the mixD) All of the above
Ans: D) All of the above
Over-vibrating, dropping from heights > 1.5m, or using excessively wet mixes causes the heavy coarse aggregates to sink and separate from the cement paste.
44. For stripping formwork of a slab spanning 4 meters, the minimum time recommended is (assuming OPC and normal weather):
A) 3 daysB) 7 daysC) 14 daysD) 21 days
Ans: B) 7 days
As per IS 456, props to slabs spanning up to 4.5m can be removed after 7 days, provided the concrete has gained sufficient strength to support its own weight.
45. Air-entraining admixtures are primarily used to:
A) Increase compressive strengthB) Decrease setting timeC) Improve resistance to freezing and thawingD) Add color
Ans: C) Improve resistance to freezing and thawing
Air entrainment creates billions of microscopic air bubbles. In freezing weather, these bubbles provide "relief valves" for expanding ice, preventing internal frost damage.
46. The specific gravity of Ordinary Portland Cement is usually around:
A) 2.65B) 3.15C) 1.44D) 7.85
Ans: B) 3.15
The specific gravity of OPC is generally tested using a Le Chatelier flask with kerosene, and the value is typically 3.15.
47. Aggregates are classified into fine and coarse zones. Sand belonging to which zone is the coarsest?
A) Zone IB) Zone IIC) Zone IIID) Zone IV
Ans: A) Zone I
IS 383 divides fine aggregates into four grading zones. Zone I represents the coarsest sand, while Zone IV represents the finest sand.
48. The compaction factor test is most suitable for concrete of:
A) High workabilityB) Medium workabilityC) Low workabilityD) Self-compacting nature
Ans: C) Low workability
The compaction factor test is more precise than the slump test for mixes with very low workability (dry mixes) often used in mass concreting or road works.
49. A construction joint is intentionally provided where:
A) Concrete pouring is temporarily stoppedB) Cracks are expectedC) Reinforcement endsD) Water pressure is maximum
Ans: A) Concrete pouring is temporarily stopped
Because continuous pouring of massive structures is impossible, construction joints are placed where concreting stops for the day and resumes later.
50. Calcium Chloride (CaCl2) is a common chemical used as an:
A) AcceleratorB) RetarderC) Air entrainerD) Waterproofing agent
Ans: A) Accelerator
Calcium chloride accelerates the setting and early strength gain of concrete. It is useful in cold weather but must be used carefully as chloride ions promote rebar corrosion.
51. Sulphate Resisting Cement is highly recommended for:
A) Marine structures and foundations in sulphate-rich soilsB) Cold weather concretingC) Plastering wallsD) Road pavements
Ans: A) Marine structures and foundations in sulphate-rich soils
Sulphates in soil or seawater attack C3A in normal cement. Sulphate Resisting Cement has a very low C3A content (<5%), making it immune to this attack.
52. In aggregate grading, a "gap-graded" aggregate implies:
A) All particles are of the same sizeB) One or more intermediate sizes of particles are missingC) Contains equal amounts of all sizesD) Only fine dust is present
Ans: B) One or more intermediate sizes of particles are missing
Gap grading intentionally omits certain intermediate sizes. This can be used for exposed aggregate finishes or specific architectural concretes.
53. Shrinkage of concrete can be minimized by:
A) Increasing water contentB) Using high cement contentC) Proper curing and reducing water contentD) Removing formwork early
Ans: C) Proper curing and reducing water content
Shrinkage is primarily caused by the evaporation of excess mixing water. Keeping water content low and providing prolonged curing minimizes shrinkage cracks.
54. Which concrete mix design method is traditionally followed in India?
A) ACI MethodB) DOE MethodC) IS Code Method (IS 10262)D) Road Note No. 4
Ans: C) IS Code Method (IS 10262)
The Indian Standard IS 10262 (updated in 2019) outlines the standard guidelines for proportioning concrete mixes in India.
55. Ready Mix Concrete (RMC) is primarily advantageous because it:
A) Is cheaper for very small jobsB) Ensures precise quality control and batching in a factory environmentC) Sets instantlyD) Doesn't require curing
Ans: B) Ensures precise quality control and batching in a factory environment
RMC is manufactured in a computerized batching plant. This guarantees exact material proportions, higher consistency, and better quality control than site-mixed concrete.
56. The setting of cement is mainly an exothermic process resulting from:
A) Evaporation of waterB) Hydration of cement compoundsC) BleedingD) Absorption of CO2
Ans: B) Hydration of cement compounds
Cement sets and hardens due to a chemical reaction with water called Hydration. This reaction releases heat (exothermic process).
57. Which property of fresh concrete determines the ease with which it can be mixed, placed, and compacted?
A) DurabilityB) ImpermeabilityC) WorkabilityD) Segregation
Ans: C) Workability
Workability is the broad term representing the internal work required to fully mix, transport, place, and compact fresh concrete without segregation.
58. The standard dimension of the cylinder used in the aggregate crushing value test is:
A) 11.5 cm diameter, 18 cm heightB) 15 cm diameter, 30 cm heightC) 10 cm diameter, 10 cm heightD) 5 cm diameter, 5 cm height
Ans: A) 11.5 cm diameter, 18 cm height
As per IS 2386, the crushing value test uses a rigid cylindrical metal measure of 11.5 cm internal diameter and 18 cm depth.
59. For underwater concreting (like bridge piers), which method is generally adopted to prevent concrete from washing away?
A) Hand mixingB) Tremie pipe methodC) Pumping from surfaceD) Throwing in bags
Ans: B) Tremie pipe method
A Tremie pipe is lowered to the bottom of the water. Concrete is gravity-fed through the pipe, displacing water upwards without allowing the concrete to wash out or segregate.
60. The purpose of using Silica Fume in High-Performance Concrete is to:
A) Increase permeabilityB) Fill microscopic voids and increase strength drasticallyC) Retard setting timeD) Act as a coarse aggregate
Ans: B) Fill microscopic voids and increase strength drastically
Silica fume particles are 100 times smaller than cement. They act as micro-fillers and highly reactive pozzolans, creating extremely dense, impermeable, and high-strength concrete.
61. Which chemical compound contributes to the ultimate (long-term) strength of cement?
A) C3SB) C2S (Dicalcium Silicate)C) C3AD) C4AF
Ans: B) C2S (Dicalcium Silicate)
C2S hydrates very slowly. While it doesn't contribute much to early strength, it provides excellent long-term strength (after 28 days to years) to the concrete.
62. While designing a mix, if the aggregate is saturated surface dry (SSD), the water absorption correction is:
A) PositiveB) NegativeC) ZeroD) Multiplied by 2
Ans: C) Zero
SSD state means the aggregate pores are full of water, but the surface is dry. It will neither absorb water from the mix nor add free water to it, so no correction is needed.
63. The primary reason for rejecting aggregates containing high levels of silt/clay is that they:
A) Increase concrete weightB) Form a coating that prevents cement-aggregate bondingC) Accelerate setting timeD) Make concrete too hard
Ans: B) Form a coating that prevents cement-aggregate bonding
Silt and clay dust coat the surface of aggregates, breaking the chemical bond between the cement paste and the aggregate, resulting in weak concrete.
64. Rapid Hardening Cement is produced by:
A) Adding more gypsumB) Increasing C3S content and finer grindingC) Mixing with fly ashD) Burning at lower temperatures
Ans: B) Increasing C3S content and finer grinding
Rapid hardening is achieved by increasing the proportion of C3S and grinding the clinker much finer, providing more surface area for rapid hydration.
65. Ultrasonic Pulse Velocity (UPV) test evaluates the quality of concrete by measuring the:
A) Rebound numberB) Speed of an ultrasonic pulse passing through itC) Depth of carbonationD) Core compressive strength
Ans: B) Speed of an ultrasonic pulse passing through it
High velocity indicates dense, solid concrete. Low velocity indicates voids, cracks, or honeycombing because sound waves travel slower in air/voids than in solid concrete.
66. A nominal concrete mix of 1:2:4 refers to the ratio of:
A) Cement : Sand : WaterB) Cement : Fine Aggregate : Coarse AggregateC) Sand : Coarse Aggregate : CementD) Cement : Water : Admixture
Ans: B) Cement : Fine Aggregate : Coarse Aggregate
Nominal mix ratios follow the format Cement : Fine Aggregate (Sand) : Coarse Aggregate (Gravel) by weight or volume. 1:2:4 roughly corresponds to M15 grade.
67. Harshness in fresh concrete implies:
A) High slumpB) Lack of cohesiveness and poor finishing propertiesC) Excessive cementD) Slow setting
Ans: B) Lack of cohesiveness and poor finishing properties
A harsh mix lacks plasticity, usually due to a lack of fine material (sand or cement) or excess coarse aggregate, making it extremely difficult to trowel to a smooth finish.
68. Stripping time of formwork for the soffits (bottom) of beams spanning over 6 meters is:
A) 3 daysB) 7 daysC) 14 daysD) 21 days
Ans: D) 21 days
According to IS 456, props to beam soffits spanning more than 6m can be safely removed only after 21 days of curing.
69. What is the approximate bulk density of normal weight concrete?
A) 1000 kg/m³B) 1440 kg/m³C) 2400 kg/m³D) 7850 kg/m³
Ans: C) 2400 kg/m³
Normal unreinforced concrete has a density of about 2400 kg/m³. RCC (Reinforced) is taken as 2500 kg/m³. (1440 kg/m³ is for cement, 7850 kg/m³ is for steel).
70. To transport concrete smoothly in a high-rise building, the most efficient method is:
A) WheelbarrowsB) ChutesC) Concrete PumpsD) Conveyor belts
Ans: C) Concrete Pumps
Concrete pumps deliver a continuous flow of concrete vertically and horizontally through pipelines, making them the most efficient method for high-rise constructions.
71. The phenomenon of continuous, gradual deformation of concrete under a constant sustained load over time is called:
A) ShrinkageB) FatigueC) CreepD) Elasticity
Ans: C) Creep
Creep is the time-dependent plastic deformation of concrete under constant sustained stress (like its own dead load), continuing over months or years.
72. Which type of cement is known for its bright, reflective aesthetic and is used in terrazzo flooring and architectural works?
A) Slag cementB) White CementC) Low heat cementD) Pozzolana cement
Ans: B) White Cement
White Portland Cement is manufactured with raw materials containing practically zero iron and manganese oxides, giving it a pure white color ideal for aesthetics.
73. Which equipment is used to compact concrete inside deep, narrow formworks like columns?
A) Surface vibratorB) Plate compactorC) Needle (Immersion) vibratorD) Tamping rod
Ans: C) Needle (Immersion) vibrator
Immersion or needle vibrators are inserted directly into the fresh concrete. They are essential for deep sections with congested reinforcement to release entrapped air.
74. Lightweight concrete is typically produced by using:
A) High-density steel aggregatesB) Lightweight aggregates like pumice, perlite, or expanded clayC) Extra waterD) Superplasticizers only
Ans: B) Lightweight aggregates like pumice, perlite, or expanded clay
Lightweight concrete relies on highly porous, low-density aggregates to reduce the dead weight of the structure, improving thermal insulation.
75. What is the typical limit for water absorption of good quality bricks used in construction?
A) < 5%B) < 20%C) < 40%D) 50%
Ans: B) < 20%
For first-class bricks, water absorption after 24 hours of cold-water immersion should not exceed 20% of their dry weight.
76. The property of cement that indicates its volume expansion after setting is known as:
A) SoundnessB) FinenessC) HydrationD) Consistency
Ans: A) Soundness
A sound cement will not undergo excessive volume expansion after setting. Unsound cement causes the concrete structure to crack and disintegrate.
77. In the Compaction Factor test, what does a ratio close to 0.95 indicate?
A) Low workabilityB) High workabilityC) Complete failure of mixD) Extreme harshness
Ans: B) High workability
Compaction Factor is the ratio of partially compacted weight to fully compacted weight. A higher value (0.92 - 0.95) indicates highly workable, flowable concrete.
78. The primary chemical reaction in Portland Pozzolana Cement (PPC) is between pozzolanic materials and:
A) Calcium Silicate Hydrate (C-S-H)B) Calcium Hydroxide (Ca(OH)2)C) GypsumD) Iron Oxide
Ans: B) Calcium Hydroxide (Ca(OH)2)
Hydration of normal cement produces C-S-H gel and Calcium Hydroxide. Pozzolans (like fly ash) react with the weak Calcium Hydroxide to form secondary C-S-H gel, boosting strength.
79. Formwork should satisfy which of the following requirements?
A) Rigid and watertightB) Easy to remove without damaging concreteC) Economical and reusableD) All of the above
Ans: D) All of the above
Good formwork must be strong enough to hold wet concrete, watertight to prevent slurry leakage, easy to strip, and durable for multiple reuses.
80. Which component of concrete occupies the maximum volume in a mix?
A) CementB) WaterC) AggregatesD) Admixtures
Ans: C) Aggregates
Aggregates (fine and coarse combined) act as the inert filler skeleton and occupy 70% to 80% of the total volume of concrete.
81. The Los Angeles machine is used to determine which property of aggregates?
A) Crushing strengthB) Specific gravityC) Abrasion resistanceD) Impact value
Ans: C) Abrasion resistance
The Los Angeles drum rotates with the aggregate and steel balls inside, creating wearing and grinding action to test the aggregate's abrasion resistance (hardness).
82. The process of converting wet raw mix into clinkers in a rotary kiln is called:
A) HydrationB) SegregationC) CalcinationD) Curing
Ans: C) Calcination
Calcination is the thermal process where calcium carbonate (limestone) breaks down into calcium oxide and carbon dioxide at high temperatures to form clinkers.
83. Low Heat Portland Cement is highly suitable for:
A) Cold weather concretingB) Mass concrete works like damsC) Quick repair worksD) Plastering
Ans: B) Mass concrete works like dams
In massive structures, the heat of hydration cannot escape, leading to thermal cracking. Low Heat Cement reduces C3S and C3A to lower the heat generation rate.
84. Under severe environmental exposure conditions (e.g., coastal areas), the minimum grade of concrete recommended by IS 456 is:
A) M15B) M20C) M30D) M40
Ans: C) M30
For 'Severe' exposure conditions to ensure durability against corrosion and chemical attack, IS 456 mandates a minimum grade of M30 for RCC.
85. A transit mixer is used for:
A) Breaking rocksB) Pumping concreteC) Transporting Ready Mix Concrete (RMC) while rotatingD) Measuring water content
Ans: C) Transporting Ready Mix Concrete (RMC) while rotating
Transit mixers are truck-mounted rotating drums that agitate or mix RMC during transit from the batching plant to the site, preventing it from setting.
86. The final setting time of Ordinary Portland Cement should not exceed:
A) 60 minutesB) 120 minutesC) 600 minutes (10 hours)D) 24 hours
Ans: C) 600 minutes (10 hours)
IS specifications state that OPC must reach its final setting time (become entirely hard and lose all plasticity) within 600 minutes.
87. What does the term "efflorescence" refer to in bricks or concrete?
A) Increase in strengthB) White powdery patches of soluble salts on the surfaceC) Sudden crackingD) Water bleeding
Ans: B) White powdery patches of soluble salts on the surface
Efflorescence is the crystallization of dissolved salts carried to the surface by moisture evaporation, leaving an unsightly white powdery deposit.
88. To create an exposed aggregate architectural finish, which chemical is applied to the formwork?
A) AcceleratorB) RetarderC) Water repellentD) Curing compound
Ans: B) Retarder
A surface retarder is painted on the formwork to delay the setting of the outermost cement paste. Upon stripping, this un-set paste is washed away, exposing the decorative aggregate.
89. Fiber Reinforced Concrete (FRC) primarily improves which property of normal concrete?

Adding steel, glass, or synthetic fibers bridges micro-cracks, significantly increasing the concrete's tensile strength, ductility, and impact resistance.

99. Honeycombing in concrete is primarily a result of:
A) High cement contentB) Improper or insufficient compactionC) Excessive curingD) Low temperature
Ans: B) Improper or insufficient compaction
Honeycombing refers to rough, pitted surfaces with exposed aggregates and voids. It happens when concrete is not vibrated/compacted enough to eliminate entrapped air.
100. In mix design, "Target Mean Strength" is kept higher than the "Characteristic Strength" to account for:
A) Evaporation of waterB) Statistical quality control variations on siteC) Shrinkage stressesD) Aggregate expansion
Ans: B) Statistical quality control variations on site
Because site conditions (mixing, materials, curing) vary, the mix is designed for a slightly higher target mean strength to ensure that 95% of field test results will still pass the required characteristic strength.
